# Template: Stakeholder Update

**Use this template by saying something like:**
> "Write a stakeholder update for March" or "Draft a monthly update email for the company"

---

## Agent Instructions

When using this template, produce a **markdown file** written to
`outputs/YYYY-MM/stakeholder-update-[period].md`.

The output should be ready to paste into an email or Slack message. Write it
as prose, not bullet-point soup. Aim for under 300 words.

### Audience
Internal stakeholders: other teams, leadership, people who aren't in day-to-day
product conversations. They want to feel informed, not overwhelmed.

### Structure to follow

```
Subject: Product Update — [Month / Period]

Hi all,

[Opening sentence: what's the headline story?]

**What shipped**
[2–4 things that went live. Focus on what they mean for users or the business,
not the technical details.]

**What's in flight**
[2–3 things actively being worked on. State what problem each solves.]

**On the horizon**
[1–2 things coming up next period, especially if they'll need input from others.]

**Heads up / asks**
[Any dependencies on other teams, upcoming decisions, or things people should
know are coming.]

[Sign-off]
```

### Tone guidance
- Confident but not salesy
- Honest about what's in progress vs what's shipped
- If something slipped or was deprioritised, name it briefly — it builds trust
- Avoid jargon that people outside the product team won't know

### What to read first

1. The roadmap file(s) for the relevant period (focus on status changes)
2. `context/strategy.md` — to frame items in company language
3. `memory/MEMORY.md` — for any standing stakeholder context or preferences
